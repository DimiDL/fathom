Graphic Novels

A graphic novel is a narrative work in which the story is conveyed to the reader using sequential art in either an experimental design or in a traditional comics format. The term is employed in a broad manner, encompassing non-fiction works and thematically linked short stories as well as fictional stories across a number of genres.
--from Wikipedia

    [close] It's not just about the pictures. Many graphic novel readers skip over them to devour the dialogue. They'll finish a story in 1/2 an hour, barely paying attention to the pictures. We can reach these folks. They don't actually hate reading, just tediously paced, archaic prose in books they were forced to read in school, such as Treasure Island. Do you know a book that has fast pacing, exciting action and great dialogue? Please share it here. Let's get them to make the transition to better, longer stories. Good examples: Bill The Galactic Hero, The Forever War, The White Mountains (The Tripod Collection) Bad examples (not bad, but not right for new readers): Treasure Island, LOTR trilogy, Crime & Punishment
